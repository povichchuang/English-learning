# Good English phrases

## Formal/Business

### On behalf of (prep.)

**Meaning**: 代表... / 以...之名

**Example**:
- On behalf of the entire team, I'd like to thank you for your support.
- I'm speaking on behalf of the company when I say this.
- On behalf of the committee, I welcome you all to the ceremony.
- Please accept this gift on behalf of your colleagues.

### Take notes (of a meeting) / Take the minutes (pv.)

**Meaning**: 記錄會議 / 做會議記錄

**Example**:
- Could you take notes during the meeting today?
- She volunteered to take the minutes.
- I'll need someone to take the minutes for tomorrow's meeting.
- He always takes notes of every meeting.

### I'd like to ... if it's okay with you (polite request)

**Meaning**: 如果您允許的話，我想...

**Example**:
- I'd like to propose a new idea if it's okay with you.
- I'd like to reschedule our meeting if it's okay with you.
- I'd like to make a few changes if it's okay with you.
- I'd like to take a break early if it's okay with you.

### We would appreciate your letting us know your preference as to... / I would appreciate if you could... (formal request)

**Meaning**: 我們希望能得知您的偏好... / 如果您能...我們將不勝感激...

**Example**:
- We would appreciate your letting us know your preference as to the delivery date.
- We would appreciate your letting us know your preference as to the payment method.
- I would appreciate if you could send me the report by Friday.
- I would appreciate if you could confirm your attendance.

## Informal/Getting Started

### Before anything else / Before all else (phrase)

**Meaning**: 首先... / 在做任何事之前... / 優先...

**Example**:
- Before anything else, let's check the schedule.
- Before all else, I want to thank everyone for coming.
- You'd better call her before anything else.
- Before anything else, we need to confirm the details.

### Kick off / Kick things off (pv.)

**Meaning**: 開始... / 啟動... / 拉開...序幕

**Example**:
- Let's kick off the project with a team meeting.
- The CEO will kick off the conference with a welcome speech.
- Let's kick things off with a quick introduction.
- The event kicks off at 9 AM sharp.

### Start something off by V-ing (phrase)

**Meaning**: 以...開始 / 透過...來啟動

**Example**:
- Let's start the meeting off by introducing ourselves.
- She started the day off by doing some yoga.
- We'll start the presentation off by showing a short video.
- He always starts the morning off by reading the news.

## Informal/Meetings

### Get off the point / Go off on a tangent (phrase)

**Meaning**: 離題 / 岔開話題 / 扯遠了

**Example**:
- You're getting off the point.
- Let's stay focused - don't go off on a tangent.
- He tends to get off the point during meetings.
- Sorry, I got off the point. Let me get back to the topic.

### Put something on the back burner (phrase)

**Meaning**: 暫時擱置 / 延後處理 / 先放一邊

**Example**:
- Why don't we put this on the back burner for now?
- Let's put the budget issue on the back burner.
- We'll put it on the back burner until next quarter.
- This project has been put on the back burner.

### I'm afraid we are getting sidetracked (phrase)

**Meaning**: 我怕我們離題了 / 我們好像扯遠了

**Example**:
- I'm afraid we are getting sidetracked.
- I hate to say it, but we're getting sidetracked.
- Let's refocus - I'm afraid we're getting sidetracked.
- I'm afraid we got sidetracked. Let's get back on track.

### Get back to where we were / Let's get back on track (phrase)

**Meaning**: 回到剛才的地方 / 回到正題

**Example**:
- Let's get back to where we were.
- Let's get back on track.
- We got sidetracked. Let's get back to where we were.
- Okay, let's get back on track.

### Summarize the discussion / Draw conclusions (phrase)

**Meaning**: 總結討論 / 得出結論

**Example**:
- Let me summarize the discussion.
- I'd like to summarize what we've discussed.
- Let me draw some conclusions from today's meeting.
- Let me summarize the key points.

### I declare the meeting closed / Let's conclude the meeting (phrase)

**Meaning**: 宣布會議結束 / 讓我們結束會議

**Example**:
- I declare the meeting closed.
- Let's conclude the meeting.
- Well, it seems that's all. I declare the meeting closed.
- Thank you all. Let's conclude the meeting here.

### Let's call it a day (phrase)

**Meaning**: 今天就到這裡 / 收工了

**Example**:
- Let's call it a day.
- It's been a long day. Let's call it a day.
- I think we've done enough. Let's call it a day.
- We've been working for hours. Let's call it a day.

## Informal/Plans

### We're (I'm) thinking of... (phrase)

**Meaning**: 我在考慮... / 我打算...

**Example**:
- I'm thinking of going to Japan next year.
- We're thinking of buying a new car.
- I'm thinking of learning Spanish.

## Formal/Announcements

### This is to announce that... / This is to let you know that... (formal announcement)

**Meaning**: 特此宣布... / 現通知... / 特此告知...

**Example**:
- This is to announce that the company will be relocating to a new office next month.
- This is to let you know that the meeting has been rescheduled to Friday.
- This is to announce that our new product will be launched on March 1st.
- This is to let you know that your order has been shipped.

## Formal/Lists

### As follows (phrase)

**Meaning**: 如下... / 如下所述...

**Example**:
- The agenda for the meeting is as follows: 1. Review of Q1 results, 2. Budget planning, 3. Team updates.
- The winners of the competition are as follows: First place - John Smith, Second place - Mary Johnson.

## Scheduling/Flexible

### Any time (day) before/after would be okay (phrase)

**Meaning**: 任何時間...之前/之後都可以 / 任何一天之前/之後都可以

**Example**:
- Any time before 3 PM would be okay for the meeting.
- Any day after Monday would be okay for the delivery.
- Any time in the morning would be okay for me.
- Any day before Friday would be okay for the submission.

## Meeting/Running a Meeting

### Please take a look at today's agenda (phrase)

**Meaning**: 請看一下今天的議程

**Example**:
- Please take a look at today's agenda.
- I've prepared the agenda - please take a look.
- Before we start, please take a look at today's agenda.

### Does everybody agree to this agenda? (phrase)

**Meaning**: 大家都同意這個議程嗎？

**Example**:
- Does everybody agree to this agenda?
- We have three items today. Does everybody agree to this agenda?
- If no one objects, does everybody agree to this agenda?

### This covers the first item (phrase)

**Meaning**: 這涵蓋第一個項目 / 這是第一個項目

**Example**:
- This covers the first item on the agenda.
- Alright, this covers the first item. Let's move on.
- That concludes the first item.

### Go on to the next item / Move on to the next topic (phrase)

**Meaning**: 進入下一個項目 / 繼續下一個主題

**Example**:
- Let's go on to the next item.
- Now, let's move on to the next topic.
- Okay, we'll go on to the next item on the agenda.
- If there are no questions, let's move on to the next topic.

### Let's listen to what sb. has to say (phrase)

**Meaning**: 讓我們聽聽某人的意見 / 聽聽某人要說的

**Example**:
- Let's listen to what John has to say.
- Let's listen to what the expert has to say.
- First, let's listen to what our client has to say.
- I think we should listen to what she has to say.

### What do you think of it? / How do you feel about it? (phrase)

**Meaning**: 你覺得怎麼樣？ / 你怎麼看？

**Example**:
- What do you think of this proposal?
- How do you feel about the new plan?
- What do you think of it?
- I've made my decision. What do you think of it?

### Do you have any problem with these agenda items? (phrase)

**Meaning**: 對這些議程項目有問題嗎？

**Example**:
- Do you have any problem with these agenda items?
- That's the plan. Do you have any problem with these agenda items?
- We've covered everything. Do you have any problem with these agenda items?

### Now, is anything missing? (phrase)

**Meaning**: 現在，有什麼遺漏的嗎？

**Example**:
- That's the agenda. Now, is anything missing?
- Before we close, now, is anything missing?
- We have a few minutes left. Now, is anything missing?
